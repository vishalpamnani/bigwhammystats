As Pep Guardiola's trophy-laden spell at Manchester City came to an end this season, an age-old discussion has been reignited across the football world.

Over the years, several managers have come and gone on both sides of Manchester, and every single one has had a massive shadow looming over them.

That shadow belongs to none other than Sir Alex Ferguson, the Manchester United manager who won 38 major trophies in a legendary 26-year managerial reign.

For years, it was inconceivable that anyone would come even close to the level of success Sir Alex had. However, in 2016, on the blue side of town, the "noisy neighbours", as Ferguson called them, appointed the man who was revolutionising the way football was played.

Guardiola joined City with an already impressive managerial record, winning a colossal 21 trophies in eight years as manager of Barcelona and Bayern Munich.

However, the question at that point in time was whether the Spaniard could enjoy similar success in the most competitive league in the world, and the last ten years have made sure that the answer to that is a resounding yes.

Now with Pep's time at City officially in the books, it is time to revisit this most difficult of questions: who is the greatest manager of all time?

## Accolades

Ferguson holds the distinction of being the most decorated manager of all time.

The Scotsman has won 49 major trophies across his career, including a record-setting 13 Premier League titles and two Champions League crowns at Manchester United.

United also became the first English side to win the treble in 1999 under Sir Alex.

However, one of the most impressive feats of Ferguson's career was winning two European trophies as manager at Aberdeen.

Sir Alex led the Scottish side to a historic victory over Real Madrid to claim the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup before beating Hamburger SV to win the Super Cup in 1983.

Unlike Ferguson, Guardiola began his career already amongst Europe's elite.

The Spaniard was entrusted with being Barcelona's first team manager after being head coach of Barcelona B for only one season.

However, Pep delivered immediately, winning the treble in his first season while implementing his own dynamic brand of possession-based football.

In his four years as Barcelona manager, Guardiola won 14 trophies, including three league titles and two Champions League trophies, building one of the greatest football teams of all time.

His success continued at Bayern Munich where he added seven more trophies to his cabinet, winning the Bundesliga three seasons in a row.

However, an elusive third Champions League evaded Pep until he joined City.

Guardiola turned Manchester City into a juggernaut, winning six Premier League titles in ten years, including a treble in 2023.

After adding an FA Cup and League Cup this season, the Spaniard has won a total of 41 major honours as a manager.

Pep also has age on his side and it may only be a matter of time before he surpasses Sir Alex's trophy haul.

## Impact on Football

However, accolades are not the only way by which a manager's impact can be measured.

Every manager aspires to mould a team in their own image, playing a brand of football that resonates with their understanding of the game.

Ferguson's 26-year reign at United saw him usher in a high-tempo, direct attacking style of football which gave birth to iconic sides such as the Class of 1992 and the legendary 2008 Champions League-winning side featuring Cristiano Ronaldo and Wayne Rooney.

The Scotsman's ability to adapt his style of play against different opponents for over two decades, while also nurturing youngsters like Ronaldo, David Beckham and Paul Scholes into world-class talents, was what made him special.

Guardiola was able to do the same when he started his career at Barcelona. His introduction of possession-based, tiki-taka football made the Spanish club a dominant force in Europe.

Under the former City boss, Lionel Messi reached the peak of his powers, with the Argentine crediting him for improving his understanding of football.

Alongside Messi, Sergio Busquets and Gerard Pique also became some of the best in their positions under Guardiola.

Pep won both his Champions League titles at Barcelona after defeating Ferguson's United in the final. The 2011 final in particular was a spectacle that saw the English club get dominated like never before.

It was a sign of things to come. After his fruitful spell at Bayern, Guardiola imposed his style of possession-based football at City.

It could be argued that Pep changed the landscape of the Premier League with his philosophy. The controlled passing style of play allowed City to run roughshod over the rest of the league for a decade.

Pep's genius also gave rise to other managers that adopted similar philosophies, such as Mikel Arteta and Enzo Maresca, who both worked with the Spaniard at City and have gone on to win major trophies with Chelsea and Arsenal.

## What Have They Said About Each Other?

Both managers have always maintained a mutual respect for one another.

While inducting Guardiola into the League Managers Association Hall of Fame 1000-Games Club, Ferguson was full of praise for the City head coach.

He said: "Pep! I am delighted to welcome you to the prestigious LMA Hall of Fame 1000-Games Club.

"Your profound love and passion for football have always been evident, and you should be immensely proud of the indelible mark you have left on the global game.

"To continue winning league titles, Champions League trophies and domestic cup competitions across Europe's three most competitive leagues is an extraordinary achievement."

Guardiola also expressed his admiration for Ferguson after the former United manager sent him a personal message following his departure from City.

"I know I had incredible success as a manager and it's nice to be there," said Pep.

"One of the biggest compliments I had was I got a message from Sir Alex Ferguson yesterday and that made me so happy.

"It's not to be humble but he is the greatest in this country. I'm so happy.

"I miss Johan Cruyff a lot, I'm happy that Sir Alex, the greatest, could see. I'm pretty sure that Sir Alex will not call us the noisy neighbours again.

"We are the neighbours, and I'm so happy that he was there to watch us."

Both managers have undoubtedly had an enduring impact on the beautiful game. The question of who's the best might go down as one of football's toughest questions.
