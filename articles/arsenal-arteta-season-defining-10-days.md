As we approach the climax of a thrilling Premier League season, Arsenal find themselves in the midst of yet another heated title race.

However, this time around, Mikel Arteta's men are closer than ever to claiming a first league title in more than 20 years. The North Londoners have finished second for the last three seasons, losing out to Manchester City and Liverpool in the process, but are now just two games away from that elusive triumph.

Arteta's men are in control of their destiny. They sit two points clear of City at the top, although Pep Guardiola's side have a superior goal difference.

Arsenal can neither afford to lose nor draw any of their remaining fixtures, in which they host a relegated Burnley side before facing 15th placed Crystal Palace at Selhurst Park on the final day.

On paper, Arteta's team should have no trouble dispatching both sides in their remaining games, having beaten them earlier in the season as well. However, nothing is ever given easily in the Premier League and Arteta will know that even the smallest of mistakes could send The Gunners' season into ruin.

The stage is set: a run of two games in ten days that will define Arsenal's season and Arteta's time at the club. Here's the breakdown of what that might look like for the Spanish manager and his side.

## Outcome One: Arsenal Win the League

Arsenal are without doubt favourites to claim their 14th league title. The win would validate the trust and patience the club's owners and fans have shown in Mikel Arteta's project.

Since being appointed in December 2019, the Spaniard has led his side to three trophies including an FA Cup and two Community Shield victories. However, the club's primary goal has always been a return to the pinnacle of English football.

In his first two full seasons in charge, Arsenal finished outside the top 4, but were establishing an identity which they had severely lacked in the seasons prior.

By his third season, Arteta had shaped the squad with his philosophy. The arrival of key players like Gabriel Jesus and Gabriel Magalhaes, along with the establishment of William Saliba and Bukayo Saka as team leaders, rejuvenated Arsenal and catapulted them to competing at the top against City.

It is the very same philosophy that has made them a key figure in every single title race since then. The addition of Declan Rice and Viktor Gyokeres among others has only solidified their status at the top.

Finally clinching that Premier League trophy would serve as the ultimate vindication for Arteta, whose style of play has been called into question over the years.

It could also lead to the start of a City-like dominance in North London. Arsenal have all the tools to remain at the top of English football, with Arteta at the helm and a growing self-belief in the squad, the beginning of a new dynasty is not as far-fetched as some would think.

## Outcome Two: Arsenal Fall Short Again

It would be an all too familiar feeling for Arsenal fans if their team were to come up short so close to the finish line.

Patience among supporters was already wearing thin at the end of last season when they finished runners-up for the third time in a row.

The addition of Eberechi Eze and Gyokeres last summer took Arteta's total spending as manager to approximately one billion pounds. A mind-boggling sum that demands more than just an FA Cup to show for it.

Arsenal failing to win the league again would deservingly bring into question whether the Spanish manager is the right man for the job. A fourth consecutive second place finish would be hard to defend given the time and finances that have been invested.

Arteta has, without a doubt, turned Arsenal into a side capable of contending for every available trophy. However, history is written by winners and if The Gunners were to fall short again, history will not look kindly upon the Spaniard's tenure in North London.
